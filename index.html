Due to this I had to give up the many sports I played and with my newfound time I developed a deeper love of gaming, further fuelling my dream of being a games developer. Professional Profile

When undertaking the quizzes necessary to creating my personal profile I undertook the three tests of the Myer-Briggs 16 personalities test, the leadership style test, and the learning style test.

My results of the Myer-Briggs test came out to be INFP, however the while the intuitive, feeling, and prospecting aspects of the test all came out heavily favouring their side of the results, my results for the introverted against the extraverted came extremely close together with 54% introverted. While I feel I agree with the results of the test as a general overview of my personality type are close to being accurate, I believe that on any given day I could end up with an ENFP result. While an INFP may be considered emotional, I think that aspect of the personality can be a benefit when working within a group or team setting. The ability to be in tune with the emotions and feelings of a group can benefit with sharing the burden of stress and anxiety when working on an important project or crunching to reach a deadline. Furthermore, my ‘fence-sitting’ between INFP and ENFP may be beneficial when guiding a group when I am tasked with a leadership role.

Continuing from my last point, my second test, the leadership test, came back with me having the result of being a “front-line” leader. One of the primary aspects of a front-line leader is their emotional intelligence within a team and their ability to be in tune with their team members to boost them up and have everyone work towards a common goal. Similar to my results with the Myers-Briggs test, I believe that the test is mostly accurate as a general sense of my leadership style and the way I work when acting as a group leader, however I personally I do not like to take the primary role as a leader, but rather would act as someone the leader can come to when in need of a second opinion or guidance when they feel lost. This is where the emotional intelligence aspects of both the INFP result and the front-line leader result I believe are accurate and are beneficial to me working within a team environment.

Additionally, through the learning style quiz it came out that I was primarily an auditory listener. I think this benefits working and leading teams greatly as it enforces connections and communication with my team members not only if I need to solve a problem that I am having, but to gather ideas in ways that may not be as formal as meetings, but rather visiting someone’s workspace and having them explain the solution to my issue or the idea that they have come up with in the team.

Project Idea

For my project idea I would like to propose and app that allows for younger people to create groups to keep track of their locations when they are going on nights out. Within the app, the users who are going out will be able to ‘check in’ to different bars and clubs that they are attending during their night out, as well as plan where they expect to go in for the night out, and the users who may not be going out will be able to look where the other users in the group plan to go, but also where they end up checking into during their night out.

My primary motivation for creating something specifically designed for going out comes from my personal experience of having friends regularly go out on Friday and Saturday nights while I was working and sending them messages such as ‘stay safe’ and telling them to ‘let me know when they get home’. Furthermore, I understand the people aged 18 to 25 tend to be more hesitant to have their parents as involved with their lives and where they are going and thus may be unwilling to install other applications such as life360. By targeting the youth with this app, I believe that they would be more willing to share this information of where they are going with their friends.

For the design of my application, I have two primary user bases in mind; those going out and those who are not. While these two userbases have specific features targeted to them, they are no user is locked from using any feature at any time.

For those who are going out the primary functions that they would be prompted to use would be planning their night, checking in at the venues that they go to, and sending alerts to the users within their group and friend list. With planning, a user can set where the plan to be during their night, as well as at what time they expect to get there and leave. These times will be auto adjusted when checking in first when they enter but also when they enter a new venue. Furthermore, by planning other users can know ahead where they can expect to find them during the night. Additionally, the check in feature allows for users to be pseudo tracked by having their location at the venue logged into the system of the app. This will set their location on a map with other users they are either friends or in a group with to see.
